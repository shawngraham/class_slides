The Right Structure For The Job

Structure Primary Objective Best Appropriate For...? Key Financial Implication
Sole Proprietorship Personal income & control Solo consultants, independent researchers, artisans monitoring a hobby-based business Owner is personally liable for all business debts
Partnership Shared risk/reward Small teams of historians/researchers pooling resources Profits and liabilities are shared amongst partners
For-Profit Corporation Wealth generating & growth (does it have to be about growth?) Scalable tech startups (e.g., dh software? See Greenhouse Studios for counter), hospitality Can distribute dividends to shareholders and raise capital through equity
Not-for-Profit Corporation Reinvesting in a mission Social enterprises that want to operate like a business but serve a social/cultural goal No share capital; all surplus is reinvested into the organization's mission
Registered Charity Public benefit Museums, archives, and educational foundations serving the public good Can issue tax-deductible receipts to donors but faces strict spending rules
Co-op Member benefit Artisans sharing a studio space / communities pooling funds for local social/economics Democratically owned; members vote

ok, so you got paid:

now there's GST

you can register for GST and get a Business Number at the same time

you must register if you earn through self-employment over $30 000 in a quarter or over four consecutive quarters

the thing you provide is taxable under GST/HST

you do NOT need to if your income is below $30 000, or your service is GST/HST exempt

Nevertheless, there are advantages to registering voluntarily:

you can claim the GST/HST back on eligible business expenses (eg office supplies, equipment)
You look more professional
You're ready for the big time

If you register, you MUST then:

charge and collect GST/HST from clients/customers
file GST/HST returns and pay the money to CRA
